# Contribution Guidelines

  ## Scope

  Submissions should present original, significant, and methodologically rigorous research relevant to the journal’s scope.

  ## Requirements

  Contributions must:

  - Clearly identify the research problem and objectives.
  - Explain the study’s originality and significance.
  - Use appropriate and reproducible methods.
  - Support conclusions with sufficient evidence.
  - Discuss limitations and practical implications.
  - Follow applicable ethical and reporting standards.
  - Cite relevant literature accurately.
  - Be written clearly and professionally.

  ## Author Contributions

  Describe each author’s role using the CRediT taxonomy:

  - Conceptualization
  - Methodology
  - Software
  - Validation
  - Formal analysis
  - Investigation
  - Data curation
  - Writing – original draft
  - Writing – review and editing
  - Visualization
  - Supervision
  - Project administration
  - Funding acquisition

  ## Research Integrity

  Authors must disclose:

  - Conflicts of interest
  - Funding sources
  - Ethics approval and informed consent
  - Data and code availability
  - Use of generative AI
  - Previous publication or simultaneous submission

  ## Submission Checklist

  Before submission, confirm that:

  - [ ] The manuscript is original.
  - [ ] All authors approved the submission.
  - [ ] The journal’s formatting requirements are followed.
  - [ ] Figures and tables are properly labeled.
  - [ ] References are complete and consistent.
  - [ ] Ethical approvals are documented.
  - [ ] Data and code availability statements are included.
  - [ ] Conflicts of interest and funding are disclosed.
